Exploring Collaborative Success of Undergraduate Students within a Cyberlearning Environment Using Gamification

Gabriel Prieto - Florida International University

Abstract

This study investigates the relationship between teamwork dynamics and academic performance by analyzing patterns of student collaboration across multiple semesters. Utilizing advanced data analysis and clustering techniques on educational data from the SEP-Cyle database, we examined how collaborative interactions evolve and impact student achievement in a Software Testing course.

Research Questions

  1. What are the patterns of student behavior and how do they relate to the team they were assigned?
  2. How does being in a team affect behavior over time?
  3. How are the student's characteristics different when we cluster them versus their original grouping?

Key Findings

Behavioral Patterns

  • Teams gradually develop more similar behaviors over time
  • Hierarchical clustering creates more cohesive and tighter-knit clusters
  • Team dissimilarity remains stable at approximately 18%

Performance Metrics

  • Clustered teams show higher grades after the 20th percentile
  • Upper percentile teams achieve higher completion rates
  • Clustered teams near the 97th percentile showed significantly higher productivity
